Findings

Conclusion

The Autoencoder outperforms Isolation Forest on this dataset, achieving a ROC AUC of 0.85 vs 0.78. The critical advantage is fraud recall: the Autoencoder correctly identifies 90% of fraudulent transactions compared to 85% for Isolation Forest.

In a fraud detection context, recall is the decisive metric. A missed fraud case (false negative) results in direct financial loss to the institution and customer. Low precision on the fraud class (0.02) is acceptable — flagging a legitimate transaction for review is a recoverable error; missing an actual fraud is not.

→ The Autoencoder's ability to reconstruct normal transaction patterns and flag high-reconstruction-error outliers makes it well-suited for this severely imbalanced dataset, where supervised methods would struggle with the 0.17% positive class rate.
